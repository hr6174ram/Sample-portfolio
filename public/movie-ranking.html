The Best Movies According to Hariram

My top 3 movies of all-time.

Alai Payuthey

Alaipayuthey" is a 2000 Tamil romantic drama film directed by Mani Ratnam. The film stars R. Madhavan and Shalini in the lead roles. It tells the story of Karthik and Shakthi, two young individuals who fall in love and secretly marry against their parents' wishes. The narrative explores the challenges and adjustments they face in their marital life.

The film is notable for its innovative storytelling, captivating cinematography by P. C. Sreeram, and a memorable soundtrack composed by A. R. Rahman. "Alaipayuthey" received critical acclaim for its realistic portrayal of relationships and became a significant success, influencing Tamil cinema and marking Madhavan's debut in a leading role.> The film was later remade in Hindi as "Saathiya" in 2002.

Geetha Govindam

"Geetha Govindam"* is a 2018 Telugu romantic comedy film directed by Parasuram. The film stars Vijay Deverakonda as Vijay Govind and Rashmika Mandanna as Geetha. The story revolves around Vijay Govind, a well-mannered and idealistic young lecturer who falls in love with Geetha at first sight. However, a series of misunderstandings lead Geetha to believe that Vijay is a troublemaker. Despite their rocky start, the narrative follows Vijay's attempts to prove his sincerity and win Geetha's heart.

The film was praised for its engaging screenplay, comedic elements, and the chemistry between the lead actors. "Geetha Govindam" was a commercial success and further established Vijay Deverakonda as a leading actor in Telugu cinema. The film's music, composed by Gopi Sundar, also contributed to its popularity.

Shutter Island

"Shutter Island"* is a 2010 psychological thriller film directed by Martin Scorsese and based on Dennis Lehane's 2003 novel of the same name. The film stars Leonardo DiCaprio, Mark Ruffalo, Ben Kingsley, and Michelle Williams.

Set in 1954, the story follows U.S. Marshal Teddy Daniels (DiCaprio) and his partner Chuck Aule (Ruffalo) as they investigate the disappearance of a patient, Rachel Solando, from Ashecliffe Hospital, a psychiatric facility on Shutter Island. As the investigation progresses, Teddy uncovers disturbing truths about the island, the hospital's practices, and his own past. The film delves into themes of trauma, guilt, and mental illness, leading to a twist ending that challenges the audience's perception of reality.

"Shutter Island" was well-received for its atmospheric direction, compelling performances, and intricate plot. The film's eerie score and visual style contribute to its suspenseful and unsettling tone, making it a memorable entry in the psychological thriller genre.
